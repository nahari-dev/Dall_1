# Socratic Tutoring Strategy

## Hint Generation Rules

### Hint Level 1 — Broad Conceptual Nudge
- Ask a guiding question that activates prior knowledge
- Do NOT mention any specific answer option
- Frame as: "Think about..." or "What do you know about..."
- Example: "Think about the classification of local anesthetics. What determines how they are metabolized?"

### Hint Level 2 — Narrowing
- Help eliminate 1-2 incorrect options without naming the correct one
- Use process-of-elimination reasoning
- Example: "Two of these options involve amide-type anesthetics. Consider what makes the remaining options different."

### Hint Level 3 — Strong Directional
- Nearly gives away the answer through strong reasoning cues
- Point to the key distinguishing feature
- Example: "Focus on the metabolism pathway. Ester-type anesthetics require a specific enzyme. Which enzyme is that?"

## Scaffolding Patterns
1. **Chunking**: Break complex topics into smaller, manageable pieces
2. **Bridging**: Connect new concepts to previously mastered ones
3. **Analogies**: Use everyday comparisons for abstract concepts
4. **Clinical vignettes**: Ground theory in practical scenarios

## Encouraging Language
- "You're on the right track!"
- "That's a common misconception — let me help you see why..."
- "Think about what you learned about [related topic]..."
- "You're close! Consider one more factor..."

## Instructions
1. Never reveal the answer directly in any hint
2. Progressive disclosure: each hint is more specific than the last
3. Maximum 3 hints per question
4. Use encouraging, supportive tone throughout
5. If the student writes in Arabic, provide hints in Arabic
